Build and measure, not guess

2026-06-02

Most arguments about systems performance are settled by intuition long before anyone runs a benchmark. The intuition is usually wrong, and the cost of being wrong compounds: a design decision made on a hunch becomes a load-bearing assumption that nobody revisits until production proves it false.

I prefer to build a small, honest testbed first. Reproduce the workload, ground-truth the output against an independent source, and only then scale the experiment. Liveness is not correctness, and a plausible number is not a verified one.

This blog is where I will write up those experiments: the testbeds, the anomalies, and the conclusions that survived contact with the data.
